# Event Summary Report: "Cloud Architect"

### 1. Event Objectives

**Cloud Architect** was a competitive team-based learning event where participants answered questions spanning cloud computing concepts and architecture design. The format was structured to:

- Test and reinforce cloud knowledge across both foundational and advanced topics.
- Create a fast-paced, engaging environment that encourages active recall and real-time problem-solving.
- Foster collaborative learning through team discussion, knowledge-sharing, and peer teaching.

### 3. Key Highlights

- **Progressive difficulty:** Questions escalated from foundational cloud concepts to complex architecture design scenarios, keeping participants engaged and challenged throughout.
- **Coverage breadth:** Topics spanned core cloud principles, service-specific knowledge, and system design — giving participants broad exposure in a single session.
- **Tie-breaker rounds:** Fast-paced elimination rounds tested quick thinking and decisive reasoning under time pressure.
- **Collaborative problem-solving:** Teams worked through questions together, turning the competition into a shared learning experience rather than just a test.

---

### 4. Lessons Learned

- **Reinforced cloud fundamentals:** Competing on a wide range of cloud topics forced me to revisit and solidify concepts I had assumed I understood — and revealed gaps I hadn't noticed before.
- **Architecture reasoning under pressure:** Answering design questions quickly strengthened my ability to think through trade-offs without over-deliberating.
- **Teamwork and communication:** Explaining my reasoning to teammates and defending answers in real time sharpened both my technical clarity and communication skills.
- **Breadth of exposure:** The diversity of questions introduced me to cloud topics outside my usual project scope, broadening my overall understanding of cloud architecture and design principles.

---

### 5. Event Experience

The Cloud Architect event struck a balance between entertainment and genuine educational value. The competitive format created energy in the room while pushing participants to actively apply their cloud knowledge rather than passively receive it. Through team discussion and exposure to a wide range of questions, I found myself consolidating what I already knew, discovering new concepts I hadn't considered, and developing a deeper appreciation for the depth and nuance of cloud architecture and solution design.
